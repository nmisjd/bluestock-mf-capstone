# Day 3 Insights Report — Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

## Overview

Day 3 focused on Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) and visualization of mutual fund datasets to identify trends, investor behavior, fund performance characteristics, and financial risk-return relationships.

The analysis was conducted using Python, Pandas, Matplotlib, and Seaborn.

---

# 1. NAV Trend Analysis

## Observation

The SBI Bluechip Fund NAV demonstrated a strong long-term upward trend between 2022 and 2026.

### Key Findings

* NAV increased steadily over time.
* Temporary market corrections were observed during volatile periods.
* Strong recovery phases indicate resilience and long-term growth potential.

### Business Insight

The fund shows characteristics of stable long-term equity wealth creation and reflects positive investor confidence in large-cap mutual funds.

---

# 2. AUM (Assets Under Management) Analysis

## Observation

SBI Mutual Fund recorded the highest AUM among the analyzed fund houses, followed closely by ICICI Prudential and HDFC Mutual Fund.

### Key Findings

* Large fund houses dominate industry AUM.
* Investor capital is concentrated among established Asset Management Companies (AMCs).

### Business Insight

Brand trust, historical performance, and distribution strength play significant roles in attracting investor assets.

---

# 3. SIP Inflow Trend Analysis

## Observation

Monthly SIP inflows showed strong and consistent growth from 2022 to 2025.

### Key Findings

* SIP inflows increased steadily over the observed period.
* Rapid acceleration in inflows was observed after 2023.
* Retail participation in mutual funds appears to be increasing significantly.

### Business Insight

The Indian mutual fund industry is experiencing growing retail investor adoption and increasing preference for disciplined long-term investing through SIPs.

---

# 4. Risk vs Return Analysis

## Observation

The scatterplot analysis revealed a clear relationship between risk and return across different mutual fund schemes.

### Key Findings

* Low-risk funds generated lower returns.
* Very high-risk funds generated the highest returns.
* Moderate-risk funds clustered around stable medium returns.

### Business Insight

The analysis confirms the classic financial principle that higher returns are generally associated with higher volatility and investment risk.

---

# 5. Investor Transaction Analysis

## Observation

T30 cities accounted for the majority of investor transactions.

### Key Findings

* Approximately 66% of transactions originated from T30 cities.
* Around 34% came from B30 cities.

### Business Insight

Metro and large urban regions currently dominate mutual fund participation, although B30 participation indicates growing financial inclusion in smaller cities.

---

# 6. Correlation Analysis

## Observation

The correlation heatmap identified important relationships between mutual fund performance metrics.

### Key Findings

* Strong positive correlation exists between 1-year and 3-year returns.
* Beta showed positive correlation with volatility.
* Sharpe Ratio demonstrated negative correlation with volatility.

### Business Insight

Funds with strong risk-adjusted performance tend to maintain lower volatility levels while delivering sustainable returns.

---

# 7. Overall Business Conclusions

The EDA results indicate that:

* The Indian mutual fund industry is experiencing sustained growth.
* Retail investor participation is increasing steadily.
* Large-cap and established funds continue to dominate investor preference.
* Risk-return relationships align with standard financial market behavior.
* Financial metrics such as Sharpe Ratio and volatility are valuable indicators of fund quality.
